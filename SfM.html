Structure-from-Motion with Generic Radial Distortion Model


Yihan Wang     Linfei Pan     Viktor Larsson     Marc Pollefeys    

Computer Vision and Geometry Group, ETHz


Abstract


we present an incremental Structure-from-Motion (SfM) pipeline that introduces a para- metric distortion model-free method for incorporating camera intrinsics. In contrast to traditional SfM approaches like COLMAP that estimate calibration parameters dependent on a pre-defined low-dimensional explicit para- metric model, we implicitly estimate the intrinsic calibration modeled by cameras’ extrinsic parameters, and our pipeline is thus robust to radial distortions and is capable of generalizing to different camera models.



Experimental results on the ETH3D benchmark and manually distorted datasets reveal that our method achieves comparable performance with state-of-the-art pipelines for regular camera systems and significantly enhances 3D reconstruction accuracy in scenarios involving severe lens distortions like those from fisheye lenses. These findings highlight the effectiveness of our explicit parametric model-free calibration in improving the flexibility and robustness of SfM systems.




Overview


Our SfM pipeline initializes with radial constraints, after upgrading to a 6 dof camera pose with implicit distortion model, a pixel-wise calibration map for each camera could be recovered or updated. Based on the completeness of this calibration map, we can choose to perform either a radial triangulation, or a standard triangulation with the intrinsics recovered. Full bundle adjustment is enabled after the triangulation procedure.



The pixel-wise calibration map retrieved from the implicit distortion method was generically modeled by a consecutive outlier assumption.
